Accomplishments

Clara Barton published a few books called “History of the Red Cross” in 1882, “The Red Cross in Peace and War” in 1899, and “The Story of My Childhood” in 1907.

Barton administered medication to the wounded and injured soldiers in battle during the American Civil War. She was known as the "Angel of the Battlefield."

In 1851, she settled in New Jersey where she established a public school that was free and publicly funded. She stopped teaching at the school because she got a different pay rate compared to other men faculty.
